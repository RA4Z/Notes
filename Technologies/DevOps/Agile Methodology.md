It is a project management approach that breaks projects into smaller, manageable phases and emphasizes flexibility, collaboration and continuous improvement.

### How it works
- **Iterative cycles:** Agile breaks projects in [[Sprints]], usually lasting 2-4 weeks
- **Incremental delivery:** Teams deliver working parts of the product at the end of each [[Sprints|Sprint]]
- **Collaboration:** Teams work closely with customers and stakeholders throughout the project
- **Adaptability:** Teams regularly review their progress, and changes their strategy based on new information or changing priorities

### Popular Agile Methodologies
- **[[Scrum]]:** A popular framework that uses sprints and specific roles to manage the development process
- **[[Kanban]]:** A method focused on visualizing workflow, limiting work in progress, and real-time communication
